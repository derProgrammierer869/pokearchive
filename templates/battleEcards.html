Battle e Cards

This section will be dedicated to the e-Reader cards for the generation 3 games. These cards were used to unlock special battles and events in the games, and were available in packs, as well as being packaged with some of the games. These cards were specifically named Battle e cards, not to be confused with the TCG e-Reader cards. 5 sets were released in total, but due to the e-Reader being discontinued, the US, Canada and Australia only recieved the first set. None of these sets were released in Europe as the e-Reader was not available to that market.

Series 1

Ruby & Sapphire Series 1 Battle e card booster pack

This was the first set realesed (Japan: June 27, 2003 -> North America: October 21, 2003 -> Australia: October 31, 2003) as part of the Battle e series. These cards were used to unlock special battles and berries in Ruby and Sapphire. 48 battle cards were included in this set, as well as 6 berry cards and 6 battle record cards. Only one card could be used at a time, using another card while another card's data was stored on the game would overwrite the previous card's data.

This set was only compatible with Ruby and Sapphire, not with LeafGreen, FireRed or Emerald versions. This was also the only set to be released outside of Japan, and cards were also packaged with copies of Ruby and Sapphire.

Trainer cards:

Trainer cards would let players send special trainer data to Ruby and Sapphire. The game is only able to store data for one trainer at a time. The player can face the imported trainer in the basement of the old man's house in Mosdeep City, and some trainers can be faced in the Battle Tower as well.

These cards show the trainers team with some of the content such as held items, or a Pokémon from the trainers party to be blurred out. The trainers have 3 Pokémon on their teams at varying levels, and the cards are colored differently based on their difficulty; green, bronze, silver, and gold. Every pack of Battle e cards contains a gold card, which has a trainer with a team of level 100 Pokémon. These are called "Target Trainers".
